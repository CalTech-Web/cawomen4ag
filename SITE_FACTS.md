## Founding Story

In 1975, several women involved in agriculture gathered in the Coachella Valley to discuss how they could educate policymakers and consumers about the needs of California agriculture. The first statewide meeting in Palm Springs drew fifty-five women who drafted the organization's initial constitution and bylaws. CWA officially began November 17, 1975.

Founding members: Beverly Sfingi, Cherry Ishimatsu, Patricia "Corky" Larson, and Jeri Taylor. Cherry Ishimatsu served as the group's first state president and celebrated her 100th birthday during the 50th anniversary Golden Weekend Celebration in La Quinta.

"Our name was carefully chosen. They chose 'for' instead of 'in' to be sure everyone who believed in the mission would join." - Sharron Zoller, former president

Notable member: Ann Veneman (CWA member) became U.S. Secretary of Agriculture under President George W. Bush.

## Mission Statement

CWA's mission is to promote and develop the interest of California women involved or interested in agriculture and to promote a strong agriculture industry in California.

"United, CWA advocates today for the economic sustainability of the diverse California agriculture community so future generations can continue to produce a healthy, diverse food supply."

"CWA is a non-partisan organization passionate about educating and promoting the importance of California agriculture to ensure families have access to a safe, nutritious, and abundant food supply."

---

## Five Core Objectives

1. To speak on behalf of agriculture in an intelligent, informative, direct, and truthful manner.
2. To keep CWA members informed on legislative activities pertaining to agriculture.
3. To join forces when the need arises to deal with agricultural issues and challenges.
4. To improve the public image of farmers.
5. To develop a rapport with consumers, educators, and governmental and business leaders in communities throughout the State.

## Programs / Services Offered

### 1. Legislative Advocacy
- Annual two-day Sacramento legislative event with lawmakers
- Federal fly-in to Washington D.C. with American Agri Women (partner coalition)
- Adopt-a-Legislator program
- Capitol Ag Day (legislature education event)
- URL: `/advocacy/`

### 2. Agricultural Education
- Agriculture in the Classroom support
- AgVenture program: 14-year-old flagship program in Lake County; 4-month deep-dive for 12 community leaders per year covering pears, walnuts, grapes, and olives (August through November)
- Farm tours for students and community leaders
- Supermarket Saturday (consumer education program)
- School and community garden funding
- Books for elementary school libraries about agriculture and science
- URL: `/education/`

### 3. Scholarships
- Chapter-level scholarships for FFA, 4-H, and college students pursuing agriculture careers
- Santa Maria Chapter alone has invested over $330,000 in scholarships
- Awards typically range from $250 to $2,000
- 10 scholarships offered at May Legislative Days
- URL: `/scholarships/`

### 4. The Compass Newsletter
- Official state publication, published 4 times per year
- Recipients include members, ag organizations, elected officials, and appointed officials
- URL: `/resources/`

### 5. CWA Foundation
- Scholarships and leadership programs for young women in agriculture
- Goal: equip next-generation ag leaders with education, experiences, and networks
- URL: `/foundation/`

### 6. Membership
- Open to anyone who believes in the mission
- Diverse membership: farmers, ranchers, bankers, lawyers, accountants, marketing professionals, consumers
- Student membership: $26.50/year
- Regular membership: $41.50/year
- Silver Donor Level: $501 to $1,000
- Platinum Donor Level: $2,000+
- URL: `/membership/`

## Key Differentiators

- Most active all-volunteer agricultural organization in California
- Founded 1975, 50 years of proven service and advocacy
- Non-partisan, open to all who support the mission
- Name includes "for" agriculture, not "in" agriculture: welcoming to everyone, not just farmers
- Membership as diverse as the industry: farmers, ranchers, bankers, lawyers, accountants, marketing professionals, and consumers
- Statewide reach across 19-20 chapters from Butte County to the Mexican border
